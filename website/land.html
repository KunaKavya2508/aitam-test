about mountain travel

Mountains are landforms that are formed by the movement of tectonic plates or by lava from volcanic eruptions. When tectonic plates collide, the rock on the Earth's surface is pushed up and over, creating mountains. Lava from volcanic eruptions piles up and cools over millions of years to form mountains.
